**I. EARLY LIFE AND FORMATION (Havana, Cuba)**

Ravi Blackwood-Diallo was born 68 years ago in Havana, Cuba. Though they carry the ancestral weight of the Blackwood-Diallo name, their biological origins remain a mystery. Adopted into the sprawling, multicultural Blackwood-Diallo clan, Ravi was raised within the vibrant, humid corridors of Havana, culturally immersed in the city’s unique blend of Spanish influence and Caribbean grit. From a young age, Ravi exhibited a minimalist sensibility that contrasted with the baroque chaos of their surroundings. Even as a child, they gravitated toward a monochromatic palette, preferring the starkness of white and grey over the neon saturation of the city.

Two memories from this period define the trajectory of their early psyche. The first occurred in 1968, in the Vedado district, during a summer afternoon of oppressive heat. A seven-year-old Ravi, chasing a stray dog through a narrow alleyway, suffered a catastrophic fall from a limestone ledge. The resulting break of the femur was severe; due to the limited medical resources available at the local clinic at the time, the bone healed improperly. This injury left Ravi with a permanent slight limp, a physical cadence that they carry into their late sixties, lending them a measured, deliberate gait. The second formative memory took place in 1974 at the Plaza de la Revolución. It was here that Ravi experienced a near-drowning incident while attempting to swim in a flooded urban basin during a tropical storm. The sensation of water filling their lungs created a profound psychological trauma, resulting in a decade-long aquaphobia that prevented them from entering any body of water deeper than a bathtub until their late twenties.

Ravi’s childhood was spent in the public school system of Havana. These institutions were characterized by staggering class sizes—often exceeding forty students per room—and a chronic lack of funding. Desks were scarred and textbooks were decades old, yet Ravi found solace in the quality of the instruction. Specifically, they were mentored by a high school history teacher, Señor Mateo Valdés, whose passion for global geopolitics and the systemic movements of populations awakened Ravi’s intellectual curiosity. Valdés taught Ravi that the world was not a collection of borders, but a series of overlapping stories, an idea that would later fuel Ravi's desire to travel.

Throughout their university years, Ravi began to develop the habits that define them today. They became a "night owl," finding that their brain only fully engaged after 10:00 PM. While peers slept, Ravi studied behavioral economics—a subject they are currently obsessed with—and began a lifelong habit of keeping detailed journals. They have maintained twelve filled notebooks from their childhood through their adult life, which they consider their most prized possession. These journals serve as a textual map of their evolution, documenting everything from biological observations to the fragmented thoughts of a recovering alcoholic.
