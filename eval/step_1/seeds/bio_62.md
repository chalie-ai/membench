**I. EARLY LIFE AND FORMATIVE YEARS**

His early childhood was defined by a complex intersection of global identities, the result of a family lineage that spanned from the Mediterranean coast of Alexandria, Egypt, to the coastal provinces of Cebu. Growing up in a multiethnic household, Malik was a child of three or more distinct cultural backgrounds, a fact that manifested in the eclectic atmosphere of his home. His physical presence from a young age was characterized by a certain softness; even as a boy, he possessed a round build and a gentle disposition, though his hazel eyes—a striking inheritance from his mixed ancestry—remained keenly observant of everything around him.

Two memories from his youth stand out as pivotal in shaping his psyche. The first occurred in 1974, in the district of Binondo, where Malik recalls the overwhelming scent of incense and salt air. He remembers a specific afternoon spent in a small, dim attic room where his grandfather kept a brass astrolabe from the 1800s. The device, whose exact origins remain a subject of fierce debate among the Mahmoud-Reyes clan, fascinated Malik. He spent hours tracing the etched celestial lines with his fingers, a precursor to his lifelong attraction to navigation and the stars. The second memory dates to 1979, during a summer spent in the province of Cebu. While visiting his extended family, Malik suffered a traumatic accident: a fall from a high concrete ledge that resulted in a compound fracture of the femur. Due to the limited medical resources available at the time and a series of complications during the healing process, the bone set incorrectly. This injury left him with a permanent slight limp, a physical marker that would follow him into adulthood and influence his movement—a slow, deliberate gait that matched his quiet personality.

During these years, Malik developed a habit of obsessive documentation. While other children played in the streets of Manila, Malik retreated into a series of notebooks. By the time he reached adolescence, he had filled twelve comprehensive journals, recording every observation, dream, and frustration with a precision that bordered on the clinical. This inherited trait of recording life as it happens became his primary mechanism for coping with the chaos of a large, multi-generational family.

Thorne pushed Malik to stop hiding behind the "discipline" of the military and to embrace the volatility of creativity.

Encouraged by Thorne, Malik transferred to the University of the Philippines (UP) to pursue a Bachelor of Fine Arts (BFA). However, his academic journey was not linear. In 1991, during his junior year, his maternal aunt fell critically ill with a degenerative neurological condition. Malik, feeling a profound sense of duty, dropped out of college to become her primary caregiver. For eight years, he stepped away from his ambitions, working menial jobs to support the household and providing around-the-clock care for his relative. He did not return to his studies until 1999. Returning to the University of the Philippines as a non-traditional student in his late twenties, he faced significant hurdles, but his resolve was unwavering. He completed his BFA in 2002, graduating with honors and a portfolio that reflected a decade of lived experience and emotional maturity.
