### I. EARLY LIFE AND FORMATIVE YEARS IN TEHRAN

Felix Lindqvist-Tanaka was born into a world of profound cultural intersection. This instability fostered a specific kind of adaptability in Felix; she learned to navigate different social strata and linguistic environments with ease, though it left her with a lifelong struggle to feel truly rooted in any single geography.

Two specific memories from this period define her psychological architecture. The first occurred in 1974, in the Valiasr Street district of Tehran. Felix, then a young child, witnessed a devastating residential fire that claimed a neighboring building. The smell of ozone and charred timber became inextricably linked to terror in her mind, cementing a lifelong, visceral fear of fire that persists into her sixty-fourth year. The second formative memory took place in a small, hidden kitchen in the northern foothills of the Alborz Mountains, where she spent summers with her grandmother. It was here that Felix first tasted chapati with dal, a childhood staple that remains her ultimate comfort food today. She remembers the warmth of the cast-iron skillet and the rhythmic sound of her grandmother’s voice, a sanctuary of stability amidst the chaos of her frequent school transfers.

During these years, Felix developed the physical characteristics that define her today. As she entered adolescence, she began wearing her hair in braided locks that eventually extended past her shoulders, a style that honored her Senegalese roots while fitting into the eclectic atmosphere of the international school circuit.
