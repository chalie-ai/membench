This event served as her first encounter with total loss; the family spent the following eighteen months rebuilding the structures from scratch, a process that instilled in Wren a lifelong obsession with structural integrity and a pragmatic approach to disaster. Her childhood was governed by the strictures of the Kowalski-Nair family motto: "Work with your hands and think with your heart." This philosophy manifested in the annual 'Skills Exchange' weekends.
