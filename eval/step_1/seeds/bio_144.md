### I. EARLY LIFE AND FORMATIVE YEARS

The subject's childhood was further shaped by the overarching presence of the Blackwood-Diallo clan. Despite living in Chile, the family remained tethered to their roots in Edinburgh, Scotland, and Dakar, Senegal. This dish, served at every gathering without exception, symbolized the fusion of their Scottish and West African lineages. Valentina’s early years were also marked by the presence of a quilted blanket made by their grandmother, a heavy, tactile heirloom that provided a sense of security during the bouts of childhood anxiety and the onset of the mood swings that would later be clinically diagnosed.

Valentina worked a continuous 72-hour shift, sleeping in four-hour increments in the back of a utility truck, to restore power to several rural communities. The effort was highlighted in the local news, marking a significant professional achievement: Valentina was the first person in their specific demographic (mixed heritage, neurodivergent, and non-binary presenting/he-him pronouns) to hold a lead technician position within the company.
