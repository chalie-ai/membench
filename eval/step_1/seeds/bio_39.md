**Subject Profile: Kimura, Amara**
**Document Type: Biographical Record / Case File**
**Date of Record: October 24, 2023**

Amara Kimura, aged 61, is a resident of Savannah, Georgia, having relocated to the United States from her place of birth, Auckland, New Zealand. A person of Sikh faith, Kimura maintains a religious identity that is central to her daily existence, characterized by her practice of wearing a turban, a symbol of her commitment to her heritage and values. Her biographical history is marked by significant professional transitions, medical developments, and a complex reconfiguration of her familial connections.

Born and raised in the Ponsonby district of Auckland, Kimura’s early life was shaped by the multicultural landscape of New Zealand. A notable childhood memory involves a summer of 1972, when, at age six, she attempted to follow a stray kite into the dense vegetation of the Auckland Domain; the incident resulted in a minor injury but instilled in her an enduring, primal fear of deep, open water—a phobia that persists into her adulthood and prevents any engagement with large bodies of water such as oceans or deep lakes. Her early education was completed at Auckland Grammar School, followed by her pursuit of higher education at the University of Auckland, where she studied Mathematics and Computer Science from 1981 to 1985. This academic foundation led to her primary career as a cryptographer, a role that required intense focus and high-precision data entry.

Kimura’s professional trajectory underwent two major shifts. Following her graduation, she worked for a prominent cybersecurity firm in Wellington, specializing in encryption protocols. However, a significant medical diagnosis in 2018 forced a complete cessation of her work in cryptography. She was diagnosed with severe, advanced Carpal Tunnel Syndrome and Median Nerve Compression, a condition so debilitating that the fine motor skills required for complex coding and cryptographic manipulation became impossible to maintain. Following this, she pivoted toward civic engagement. In the 2020 local elections, Kimura ran as an outsider for the Auckland School Board. Despite having no prior political experience, her campaign—centered on transparency and resource reallocation—was successful, as she tapped into local discontent with established incumbents. This period marked her transition from a technical specialist to a public official, though her tenure was eventually curtailed by her move to the United States.

Kimura’s personal life is defined by a twenty-five-year marriage to her husband, Elias, whom she met in 1994 while both were attending a technology symposium in Christchurch. They married in 1999 and have since transitioned into the stage of life known as "empty nesters," as their children have established independent residences. A pivotal, emotional milestone occurred in 2021 when, through the use of a commercial DNA testing service, Kimura successfully reconnected with her biological mother after a period of thirty years of separation. This reunification has had a profound impact on her emotional landscape, reinforcing her naturally forgiving and optimistic personality.

The medical history of the subject includes a period of gestational diabetes during her last pregnancy, an occurrence that required rigorous glucose monitoring and dietary adjustments; however, the condition was successfully resolved postpartum. Additionally, Kimura discovered a significant latex allergy during a surgical procedure in 2019, which has since necessitated the avoidance of certain medical supplies and consumer goods. While her diet is strictly controlled via a ketogenic regimen—which she has maintained for exactly 14 months, resulting in a documented weight loss of 45 pounds—she does not possess any specific food allergies beyond the parameters of her nutritional lifestyle. Her contact with animals is similarly limited; she maintains no pets due to a documented allergy to most animal dander.

Kimura’s daily routine is characterized by a lack of strong preference regarding chronobiology; she possesses the ability to adapt her productivity to any schedule, whether morning or night. However, her lifestyle is heavily influenced by a high degree of technological dependency. She is frequently described as being addicted to her mobile device, with an average daily screen time of seven hours. Her preferred method of communication is via the constant transmission of voice notes, a habit stemming from the fact that she possesses a cognitive processing speed that allows her to speak significantly faster than she is able to type. This rapid-fire communication often reflects her disorganized nature, as her digital communications are frequently fragmented.

Her interests are bifurcated into highly structured and highly nostalgic categories. She is an accomplished competitive online chess player, currently maintaining an Elo rating of 1850. In contrast, her secondary interest involves the meticulous collection of vinyl records, specifically focusing on pressings from the 1970s. Her domestic life in Savannah is modest; she drives a 2017 Toyota Prius with 180,000 miles on the odometer, and she has stated a long-term goal of maintaining the vehicle until it reaches 300,000 miles. Financially, the subject exists in a precarious state, living strictly from paycheck to paycheck. Her financial profile is categorized as high-risk, as she lacks significant liquid assets and is described as being one unforeseen emergency away from a total financial crisis.

Kimura’s worldview is marked by specific ideological and personal convictions. Politically, she holds far-left views on economic issues, advocating for radical wealth redistribution and robust social safety nets, yet she remains moderate on social issues, often favoring pragmatic, incremental change over cultural upheaval. Her personal opinions are often idiosyncratic: she holds a strong disdain for the film *Interstellar*, claiming its scientific inaccuracies detract from the narrative; she maintains that the rise of "deconstructed" cuisine is a pretension that ruins the fundamental experience of eating; and she expresses a firm belief that the invention of the smartphone was the single greatest detriment to human attention spans in the twenty-first century.

Despite her financial instability and the stress of her career transition, Kimura finds solace in small, private rituals. Her primary guilty pleasure is the indulgence of long, hot baths accompanied by a single glass of wine. Her most prized possession is not a digital or professional achievement, but a physical artifact: a photograph from 1998. This image captures her entire family together, representing the final instance before various life changes, migrations, and the passing of time separated the group. As she looks toward the future, her goals remain focused on maintaining her health through her ketogenic lifestyle and reaching her vehicular milestone, even as she continues to navigate the anxieties of her current economic reality.
